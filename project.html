Background

Liberia, the oldest African country founded by the American Colonization Society at the end of slavery in America in the 1800's, is the most underdeveloped nation among the nations of West Africa. It had the highest rate of illiteracy (85%) before the civil wars in the 1990's. There were fewer high schools than primary schools, and few vocational schools and colleges. At that time, the few secondary schools and institutions could not accommodate the student population.

While this problem existed, the civil unrest which lasted for about fifteen (15) years damaged every life-supporting infrastructure of which the education sector is a part, and this leads us to starting all over. Therefore, with the establishment of Royal Christian Institute, which will be modeled after some of the best private schools in Liberia, is expressly intended to be affordable and available to members of one of the poorest communities in the city of Monrovia.

A Problem, The Solution

As the country puts bits and pieces together in order to get the education system back on course, the desire for learning grows more and more, but learning facilities are limited. This condition, coupled with poverty, deprives a huge number of children and youth the opportunity of learning yearly. As in most parts of Monrovia itself - about 84% of the population lives below the international poverty line of $1.90 a day (2015), and only 56% of the adult population is literate. Even that percentage of literacy is challenged by the fact that most did not complete high school.
